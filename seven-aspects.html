Sources–Resources

This aspect is about what supports and sustains us.

What it means

Sources–Resources includes the people, ideas, traditions, practices, knowledge, tools, and forms of care that help a person or community continue growing. It asks us to notice what we draw from, what we rely on, and what we can share with others.

How it lives

It also invites us to think beyond resources as possessions. A conversation, a story, a skill, a relationship, or time offered with care can all become meaningful sources of support.

Reflection

What sustains you, and what are you able to offer others?

Presence–Being

This aspect is about making room to notice what is actually here.

What it means

Presence–Being invites us to slow down before reacting, performing, or trying to solve everything. It is the practice of paying attention to ourselves, to other people, and to the moment we are sharing.

How it lives

Being present does not mean becoming passive. It creates the awareness needed to respond with greater clarity, care, and intention.

Reflection

What becomes visible when you slow down long enough to notice?

Engagement

This aspect is about moving from observation into relationship and participation.

What it means

Engagement begins when we choose to meet what is happening rather than remain distant from it. It can take the form of listening, asking questions, joining a conversation, showing up consistently, or working with others toward something meaningful.

How it lives

The goal is not constant activity. Meaningful engagement is responsive. It grows from attention, relationship, and a willingness to participate honestly.

Reflection

Where are you being invited to participate more fully?

Realizing Divine Human Potential

This aspect begins with the belief that every person carries unique value and possibility.

What it means

Realizing Divine Human Potential is not about becoming perfect or proving our worth. It is about recognizing the brilliance, capacity, imagination, and care that already exist within people.

How it lives

It asks what conditions allow those qualities to emerge, and how individuals and communities can help one another become more fully themselves.

Reflection

What becomes possible when people are trusted to bring their full selves?

Service–Offerings

This aspect is about allowing our gifts and experiences to become useful.

What it means

Service–Offerings looks at what each person can bring into the world. This may be a skill, an idea, care for another person, a creative practice, leadership, hospitality, or the willingness to help something grow.

How it lives

Service is not treated as sacrifice for its own sake. It is a relational exchange where what we offer is shaped by listening, responsibility, and the real needs of the people involved.

Reflection

What can you offer in a way that is useful, honest, and life-giving?

Nature–Nurture

This aspect explores the conditions that allow life to grow.

What it means

Nature–Nurture includes our relationship with the natural world, but it also asks how people, ideas, relationships, and communities are cared for over time.

How it lives

Growth cannot always be forced. It depends on attention, patience, healthy environments, and respect for natural rhythms. This aspect invites us to consider what we are cultivating and what kind of conditions we are creating around it.

Reflection

What in your life needs to be protected, nourished, or given more time?

Community–Inclusion

This aspect is about creating spaces where people can genuinely belong and participate.

What it means

Community–Inclusion goes beyond inviting people into a space that has already been defined for them. It asks whether different voices are heard, whether people can influence what happens, and whether difference is treated as something valuable.

How it lives

A living community is shaped by the people within it. Inclusion means making room for those people to contribute, question, lead, learn, and help create what comes next.

Reflection

Who is able to belong, participate, and shape the spaces you are part of?
